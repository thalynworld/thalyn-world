A world that feels like somewhere.

The tools that exist produce worlds that look like something — but rarely feel like anything. The terrain is technically correct. The assets are high quality. But walk through it and the space says nothing. There is no sense of shelter, no sightline that draws the eye, no clearing that invites you to stay.

Every system in Thalyn is built around a single question: does this feel the way it should? Terrain resolves around the emotion you choose. Assets land where spatial psychology says they belong. Light, atmosphere, and path behave as designed elements — not defaults. The result is a world with intent.

This is not a faster way to make a generic world. It is a different understanding of what a world is for.
