Emotionally Coherent Procedural World Generation

Environmental Psychology as a Generative Framework

Full document available as a formatted Word file for citation and archival purposes.

Download .docx

Abstract

This disclosure describes a method and system for procedurally generating three-dimensional virtual environments whose spatial composition, asset placement, and atmospheric properties are driven by parameters derived from empirical environmental psychology research. The core innovation is the use of multi-dimensional emotional profile vectors — parameterised from validated human perception studies including prospect-refuge theory, vastness-awe research, and spatial density psychology — as the primary control surface for an automated world generation pipeline.

Unlike prior approaches that use aesthetic heuristics, noise-based randomness, or manual artist direction, the system described here encodes psychological research findings directly as generative constraints. The result is environments in which emotional response is a designed output rather than an emergent side effect.

1. Field of the Disclosure

This disclosure relates to procedural content generation (PCG) for real-time three-dimensional environments, and specifically to the application of environmental psychology theory as a computational framework for driving generative placement, terrain topology, atmospheric rendering, and scene composition. Applications include but are not limited to: game development tooling, virtual reality environment generation, architectural visualisation, spatial design platforms, educational simulations, wellness and therapeutic virtual environments, and creative tooling for non-technical users.

2. Background

Procedural content generation for virtual environments has historically relied on two broad approaches: (a) noise-based terrain and placement systems that produce geographically plausible but emotionally neutral results, and (b) manual artist-directed workflows that achieve emotional resonance through skilled labour at high cost. Neither approach systematically encodes human emotional response as a generative objective.

A substantial body of empirical research in environmental psychology has established reliable, experimentally validated relationships between spatial properties and human emotional response — including prospect-refuge theory (Appleton, 1975), Kaplan and Kaplan's Attention Restoration Theory (1989), Russell's circumplex model of affect (1980), and work on vastness and awe (Keltner and Haidt, 2003). These relationships have been extensively validated in physical environments but have not, prior to this disclosure, been systematically applied as a computational framework for procedural generation of virtual spaces.

Existing procedural generation tools that incorporate aesthetic parameters do so through artist-curated heuristics not grounded in or validated against psychological research. The distinguishing characteristic of the system disclosed here is the use of research-validated psychological parameters as the primary and authoritative generative control surface — not supplementary aesthetic polish, but the foundational driver of every spatial decision.

3. Disclosure of the System

3.1 Multi-Dimensional Emotional Profile Vectors

The system encodes intended emotional outcomes as a vector in a multi-dimensional emotional parameter space. Four primary dimensions are used: Tension (psychological arousal and threat perception), Energy (activation and dynamism), Warmth (safety, comfort, and social affiliation), and Mystery (curiosity, uncertainty, and concealment). Each dimension is a normalised scalar value.

These dimensions are grounded in established psychological research. Tension maps to the threat-detection axis of prospect-refuge theory; Energy corresponds to activation levels in Russell's circumplex model; Warmth corresponds to the valence dimension with reference to environmental comfort research; Mystery corresponds to Kaplan's four-factor spatial preference model. The emotional profile vector serves as the root input to all downstream generation decisions — terrain topology, asset selection, placement density, weather, lighting, and atmospheric properties are all derived from this vector.

3.2 Physics-Based Terrain Generation with Emotional Constraints

Terrain topology is generated using a physics-based simulation whose physical parameters are derived from the emotional profile vector. High Tension profiles produce irregular, high-gradient topology with sharp transitions. High Warmth profiles produce gentle, rounded topologies. High Mystery profiles introduce concealment features such as enclosed valleys and variable-height ridgelines that obstruct long sightlines.

Hero pedestal structures — localised elevated or depressed terrain features serving as spatial anchors — are generated by applying shaped forces to the simulated surface. Their position, scale, and intensity are determined by the emotional profile. This approach guarantees surface continuity and walkability as a mathematical property of the simulation, not a post-processing correction.

The specific algorithmic methods by which the physics simulation achieves its results — including solver architecture, convergence criteria, and numerical methods — are implementation details not disclosed herein and are protected as proprietary trade secrets. See Section 3.7.

3.3 Prospect-Refuge Scoring for Asset Placement

Asset placement is governed by a multi-factor scoring system. One factor is a prospect-refuge score computed for each candidate placement position. The prospect score measures the degree to which a position affords wide visual access; the refuge score measures enclosure and concealment. The optimal prospect-refuge ratio for a given placement candidate is determined by the emotional profile vector — derived from Appleton's published empirical framework, not from aesthetic preference.

The scoring system combines prospect-refuge scoring with: terrain physical validity, biome ecological appropriateness, inter-asset semantic relationships (species clustering, canopy spread, ecological succession), and compositional balance. Relative weights among these factors are modulated by the emotional profile. Specific weight values and scoring formulae are not disclosed herein.

3.4 Hero Pedestal Emotional Influence Zones

Each hero pedestal radiates concentric emotional influence zones. The emotional profile active within each zone is an interpolated blend between the hero's localised emotional intent and the scene-level global emotional profile. Asset placement within each zone inherits this blended profile rather than the raw global profile, creating spatially coherent emotional gradients across the generated scene.

3.5 Atmospheric and Rendering Parameter Derivation

Atmospheric properties — weather type and intensity, sky conditions, fog density and colour, lighting temperature and direction, particle effects, ambient sound — are derived from the emotional profile via a rule-based mapping system. The mapping rules are applied as soft constraints with stochastic variation seeded from a world seed parameter, preserving full determinism while introducing natural variation.

3.6 Multi-Platform Deterministic Output via Manifest Architecture

The generation pipeline produces a platform-agnostic manifest describing the complete spatial composition of the generated world: terrain heightfield data, biome region maps, per-asset placement positions and orientations, water body specifications, atmospheric parameter sets, and emotional metadata. The same manifest drives rendering across multiple target platforms via platform-specific adapter layers. Generation is seeded and strictly deterministic: the same world seed combined with the same emotional profile parameters always produces an identical manifest.

3.7 Server-Side Architecture and IP Posture

The generation pipeline executes entirely on controlled server infrastructure. Client applications receive the output manifest; they do not receive or execute the generation algorithms. This architecture ensures that specific implementation details of the emotional scoring system, terrain solver, placement brain, and manifest construction pipeline are not derivable from client-side inspection. The concepts disclosed in this document are published at a level of abstraction sufficient to establish prior art. The implementation layer below that abstraction remains proprietary.

5. Scope of this Prior Art Disclosure

This disclosure is intended to establish prior art preventing the grant of patents covering any of the following classes of concepts:

  1. The use of environmental psychology research parameters — including but not limited to prospect-refuge scores, affect-model dimensions, and preference-model factors — as generative control surfaces for procedural 3D environment generation.
  2. Systems in which terrain topology is generated by physics-based simulation whose parameters are derived from multi-dimensional emotional profile vectors grounded in environmental psychology research.
  3. Placement scoring systems for procedural asset placement in which prospect-refuge theory is used to compute per-position suitability scores, and in which the target prospect-refuge ratio is determined by a psychological emotional profile parameter.
  4. Multi-zone emotional influence regions radiating from hero spatial anchor points, in which asset placement within each zone inherits an emotionally-blended profile interpolated between the local hero intent and the global scene emotional profile.
  5. Atmospheric and rendering parameter derivation systems in which weather, lighting, fog, colour palette, and particle properties are mapped from psychological emotional profile parameters using research-grounded rule systems.
  6. Platform-agnostic manifest architectures for procedurally generated environments in which the same generation output drives multiple rendering targets via adapter layers, with emotional metadata preserved in the manifest.
  7. Deterministic seeded procedural world generation systems in which a world seed combined with a psychological emotional profile vector uniquely and reproducibly determines the complete spatial composition of a generated environment.
  8. Server-side generation architectures in which emotional scoring and terrain generation execute remotely and client applications receive only a platform-agnostic output manifest, such that implementation details are not derivable from client-side inspection.

Nothing in this disclosure restricts the independent development of any of the above concepts by third parties. The purpose is exclusively to place these concepts in the public domain as prior art, preventing exclusive patent rights from being granted to any party — including the publisher of this document.
